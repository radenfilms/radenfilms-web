WHAT CASTING DIRECTORS WANT IN A HEADSHOT

Your Headshot is Everything

It's your first (and sometimes only) chance to make an impression, especially if the casting director hasn't met you.

Looks Like You

The headshot must accurately represent you as you are today, not heavily edited, outdated, or overly glamorous.

Well-Lit & Natural

Good lighting (often natural light) is essential; it should clearly show your features without looking artificial.

Avoid Over-Stylization

No extreme makeup, fashion photography vibes, or dramatic posing. Keep it honest and professional.

Variety Without Repetition

Have 4-6 strong images showing a range of looks (e.g., with and without glasses, hair variations, facial hair options). Avoid multiple shots that look nearly identical.

Let Casting Directors Cast You

Don't try to look like a character; keep your expression neutral and natural so casting professionals can envision you in multiple roles.

Update Regularly

Change your headshots every couple of years to reflect your current look. Get rid of outdated graduation photos.

Ask for Feedback

Your agent or industry professionals can provide valuable input on which headshots best represent you.

The most authentic version of you!
